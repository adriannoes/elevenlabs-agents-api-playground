# Healthcare demo — Headache in telephone triage

**Fictional** first-contact guidance when the patient reports **headache**.

Tension-type headache is common in workplace settings and often improves with rest, hydration, and occasional use of common analgesics **when already prescribed** by the treating clinician. Thunderclap-onset headaches associated with focal neurological deficits, sudden stiff neck, or sudden vision changes are potentially severe and should be treated in this flow only as a reason to direct the caller to **emergency services**, without downplaying risk.

For this demo, when headache is described as unilateral pounding pain with photophobia without a typical aura, mentioning **Neurology** or **General Internal Medicine** follow-up may be framed as **organizational** routing — always with non-diagnostic language.

Avoid repeating sensitive data aloud; confirm identity only when needed for scheduling logistics. Reinforce limits of digital channels and AI: vague or progressive symptoms require an in-person human evaluation.
