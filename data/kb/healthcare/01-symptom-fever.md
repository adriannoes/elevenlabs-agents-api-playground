# Healthcare demo — Triage protocol: fever

This document supports the first level of triage when callers report **fever**, using **fictional** flows for demonstration only.

Persistent high fever can accompany acute respiratory infections, gastroenteritis, or other common primary-care presentations. For this demo, assume that high fever for more than 48 hours without improvement with usual antipyretics, or with reduced general condition, merits **priority in-person evaluation** at an urgent or emergency service per the fictional contracted network.

Patient-facing language: explain that fever is a **symptom**, not a diagnosis; encourage adequate hydration and monitoring for alarm signs such as confusion, new rash, severe abdominal pain, or sudden shortness of breath — situations where **immediate** emergency referral should be recommended without attempting remote diagnosis.

For specialty routing in this demo: when high fever for two or more days is associated with productive cough or mild pleuritic chest discomfort, suggest assessment in **General Internal Medicine** or **Pulmonology** depending on availability in the fictional health network. Never replace an in-person medical evaluation.
